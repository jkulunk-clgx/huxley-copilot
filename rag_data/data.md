# 1. Interview & Participant Metadata

- **Interviewee:** Jerry
    
- **Interviewers:** John, Rohnak Habeeb, Anne
    
- **Subject:** Real estate agent workflows, day-to-day operations, marketing/branding efforts, and feedback on software tools (CoreLogic, Matrix, OneHome).
    

# 2. User Persona & Background

- **Demographics:** 50 years old.
    
- **Location:** Dallas, Texas. Sells across the entire North Texas region.
    
- **Role:** Independent Real Estate Agent. He is entirely hands-on and does not use a team or an assistant. He handles everything from administrative paperwork and negotiating repairs to physical tasks like taking out the trash at listings.
    
- **Brokerage:** Currently aligned with Keller Williams (works in an office of ~50 agents, though only about 7 attend regularly). Previously worked for home builders and Berkshire Hathaway.
    
- **Career Timeline:** Started selling houses at age 23. Got licensed in 2009 during the Great Recession while working for builders. Fully transitioned to focusing solely on his license in 2017.
    
- **Working Style:** Highly organized, systematic, and self-described as "analog." He is direct, honest with clients (will tell them if a house is a "dump" or in a bad neighborhood), which builds immense client trust. He believes in providing constructive feedback and contributing to solutions rather than just complaining about industry tools.
    

# 3. Business Performance & Throughput

- **Transaction Spectrum:** Extremely diverse. Sells everything from $10,000 vacant lots up to $1.4 million homes. Average sales price is relatively low for the Dallas marketplace, meaning his high volume requires a massive number of transactions.
    
- **Production Volume (Recent Years):**
    
    - Two years ago: $17 million in volume.
        
    - Last year: $12 million in volume.
        
    - Current year: On pace to hit $17 million again (already matched last year's total).
        
- **Future Goals:** Initial target is reaching $23 million in annual volume. Wants to streamline his business to run mostly from his phone within the next 20 years to improve quality of life.
    
- **Peak Workload:** Can handle up to 7 transactions in a single month. During peak times, he works continuously from 9:00/10:00 AM to 10:00/11:00 PM without breaks to eat or rest.
    

# 4. Daily Workflow & Operations

- **Morning Routine:** First task is opening his computer and launching all essential browsers (Matrix, ZipForms, etc.). Evaluates fresh listings manually every morning despite having auto-saves, ensuring nothing is missed.
    
- **Triage Phase:** Usually out of the office doing showings between 10:00 AM and 7:00 PM. Returns to the office around 7:00 PM for "triage" (handling critical immediate items like offers). Routine admin and follow-up work often gets ignored during busy periods due to exhaustion.
    
- **The "War Room" (Analog Tracking):** - Relies heavily on visual, physical organization.
    
    - Keeps physical bundles/folders for every active file and prospect in a specific box.
        
    - Uses a physical whiteboard for tracking upcoming closings.
        
    - This analog system strictly dictates his schedule, reminding him of daily deliverables and heavily mitigating his business anxiety.
        

# 5. Technology Stack & Software Usage

- **CoreLogic Products Used:**
    
    - **Matrix:** Used extensively, at least 2 hours every single day. Considers it indispensable. Views it highly favorably (compares its reliability to McDonald's—it is consistently available, fast, and lacks buffering).
        
    - **Realist:** Used daily.
        
    - **Listing Data Checker:** Triggers every time he enters a listing.
        
- **CoreLogic Products Avoided/Disliked:**
    
    - **OneHome:** Strongly disliked by his clients. Transitioning to it caused clients to ask to be unsubscribed. Jerry felt it looked "cartoonish," impersonal, and gave clients the impression they were being abandoned to a generic portal.
        
- **CoreLogic Products Unfamiliar With:** MLS Touch, MLS Client app, Clarity, ePropertyWatch.
    
- **Transaction/Forms Management:** ZipForms and Authentisign. He views these as a "necessary evil" and heavily criticizes them for being overly complicated and requiring repetitive data entry for standard contracts.
    
- **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):** - **Does NOT use standard CRMs** (e.g., Sage CRM).
    
    - **Reason for Avoidance:** They are overly complex, require too much data entry, and there is industry-wide fear/mistrust that vendors or brokerages will misuse or retain permanent access to agent prospect data.
        
    - **Workaround:** Uses an analog spreadsheet called his "Christmas Card List," consisting of every buyer/seller he has ever worked with.
        

# 6. Key Pain Points & Frictions

- **Client Communication via Matrix/OneHome:** Matrix lacks modern sharing capabilities. Younger clients want listings via text or Facebook Messenger. To do this, Jerry must email listings to himself, copy the link on his iPhone, and manually text it to the client.
    
- **Tracking & Analytics:** When using his workaround (sending links directly from his personal Gmail to maintain a personal touch), he loses all visibility. He cannot see if a client opened the email, clicked the link, or favorited a specific property. He needs this data to prioritize follow-ups.
    
- **Matrix Mobile Experience:** Attempted to use Matrix on his iPhone but found it highly dysfunctional compared to older iterations (e.g., on his old Blackberry).
    
- **Administrative Burden:** Follow-up and general admin are the weakest points for most realtors. Jerry struggles to maintain his 6-11 touchpoints per year with his "Christmas Card List" due to time constraints, costing him potential sales.
    
- **UI Clutter:** Noted that the layout of listing information on Matrix can sometimes be "blurring" or hard to parse when scanning 10-20 listings at a time.
    

# 7. Feature Requests & Product Opportunities

- **Date Range Search (High Priority):** Desperately needs the ability to search for closed listings within a very specific historical time bracket (e.g., between 2017 and 2019) to justify pricing comps for past clients.
    
- **Price Per Square Foot Search (High Priority):** Needs a specific parameter to search properties by a tight price-per-square-foot window. Currently spends up to 2 hours manually filtering hundreds of listings to find these comps.
    
- **Seamless Cross-Platform Export:** A built-in feature to push Matrix listings directly to SMS, Facebook Messenger, and native email without leaving the platform.
    
- **Read Receipts / Client Intent Tracking:** A dashboard or notification system to see if a sent link was opened, allowing him to gauge if a client is "live" or a dead lead.
    
- **Co-Branded / Personalized Client Portal:** If OneHome (or a replacement) looked exactly like an app Jerry built himself, he would use it. It must retain his personal brand to prevent the client from feeling handed off.
    
- **"Dumbed Down" CRM:** Wants a hyper-simple CRM that runs in the background. It should only require a name and email, and automatically ping clients 6-11 times a year with no complex setup.
    
- **Integrated Forms:** Wishes Matrix had a built-in signature/form capability so property data could flow seamlessly into residential contracts without using ZipForms.
    

# 8. Branding & Marketing Strategy

- **Significance:** Rates the importance of branding as 7 out of 7. It is integrated into everything he touches.
    
- **Visual Consistency:** Maintains strict consistency with his professional photo, signage, and the Keller Williams/Berkshire color palettes. Hired a graphic designer specifically to build a custom banner for his Matrix printouts and emails.
    
- **Social Media:** - **YouTube:** Has ~160 videos and ~300 subscribers. Actively generates inbound leads from specific complex walkthroughs.
    
    - **TikTok:** Just joined reluctantly because industry peers reported closing sales from the platform.
        
    - **Print:** Recently featured in a prominent industry publication, _Real Producers_, which he leveraged heavily for a comprehensive branding update.
